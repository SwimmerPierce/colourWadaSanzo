# Wada Sanzo and *A Dictionary of Color Combinations*

## Sanzo Wada (1883–1967)
Sanzo Wada was a pioneering Japanese artist, teacher, and costume/kimono designer. Active primarily during the early to mid-20th century, he played a crucial role in shaping Japan's understanding of color. Wada was uniquely positioned at a time when Japan was rapidly modernizing; his work successfully bridged traditional Japanese aesthetic sensibilities with Western-influenced color theory and studies. He was highly acclaimed in his field, even receiving an Academy Award for Best Costume Design for the 1953 film *Gate of Hell*.

## *A Dictionary of Color Combinations*
*A Dictionary of Color Combinations* is a modern publication—most notably the popular 2010 edition by Seigensha Art Publishing—that distills Wada's extensive research into a highly practical reference guide. 

### Origins
The book is based on Wada's original magnum opus, *Haishoku Soukan* (The Complete Collection of Color Combinations), which was originally a six-volume masterwork published between 1933 and 1934 during the early Showa era. 

### Content and Legacy
- **348 Color Palettes:** The modern dictionary curates 348 striking color combinations from Wada's original research, ranging from 2-color to 4-color palettes.
- **Practical Application:** Rather than serving merely as an academic or theoretical text, the book is designed for practical, everyday use. It presents the palettes along with modern color values (such as CMYK, which have since been supplemented with RGB and HEX codes in various digital open-source adaptations).
- **Enduring Influence:** The book is widely regarded as an indispensable and inspiring resource for contemporary graphic designers, illustrators, interior decorators, and fashion designers. It demonstrates that Wada's early 20th-century color sensibilities remain incredibly relevant, vibrant, and aesthetically pleasing today.
